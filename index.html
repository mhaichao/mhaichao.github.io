About Me

I am a Post-doctoral researcher at the Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory. My goal is to visualize large-scale data from complex scientifc domains and develop interactive tools for the exploration and analysis.

Recent News

July 2020

I started a new position at the Austrian Institute of Technology and joined the MARILIA project. Our software system Adenita for the interactive 3D modelling and visualization of DNA nanostructures was published in the Nucleic Acids Journal as a full paper. Adenita heavily relies on advanced multiscale visualization and scale-adaptive modifications, which we I published at IEEE SciVis 2017. It has always been my goal to publish my visualization reserach in the domain science as well. This tools is now being used by several research groups around the world that focus on DNA-based nanostructures.
